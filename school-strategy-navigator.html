School Strategy Navigator

A structured tool for school leaders to analyse, articulate, and strengthen their school's strategy — using three complementary frameworks.

🔍 Zenger's Strategic Theory
📊 World Management Survey
🧩 Business Model Canvas
How it works: You'll work through three frameworks sequentially. Each illuminates a different dimension of your school's strategy — what makes it distinctive (Zenger), how well it is managed (WMS), and how it creates and sustains value (BMC). At the end, you receive a synthesised strategic profile with priority recommendations. Allow 30–45 minutes for a thoughtful assessment.
🔍
Zenger's Strategic Theory
Framework 1 of 3 · Strategic Identity
Todd Zenger argues that lasting value comes from a coherent corporate theory — a unified logic connecting your unique assets to real value creation. Rather than chasing tactics, this framework asks: what is the underlying theory that makes your school distinctively valuable?

Reflect on each of the three dimensions below. These questions are about articulating what you already intuitively know. There are no wrong answers.
🔍 Insight — Your Unique Assets
"What do we uniquely have, know, or do that others cannot easily replicate?"
Consider: distinctive teaching approaches, faculty expertise and culture, community trust and relationships, physical or digital assets, intellectual methods, student community, historical track record.
🔭 Foresight — Anticipating the Future
"What trends do we see coming that others may be underestimating?"
Consider: shifts in what students need to be career or life-ready, demographic changes, family expectations, technology in learning, policy changes, community evolution, changes in competition for students or staff.
🔗 Cross-Sight — Connecting the Dots
"How do our assets combine to create value greater than their individual parts?"
Consider: complementarities between your capabilities, how resources reinforce each other, what unique combinations you have built that competitors couldn't easily replicate, the 'flywheel' effects in your model.

World Management Survey — Education
Framework 2 of 3 · Management Practices
The World Management Survey (Bloom et al.) measures management practices in schools across four domains. Each practice is scored 1–5: from informal/weak (1) through developing (2–3) to best practice (5). Research consistently shows that stronger management practices correlate with better student outcomes. Be honest — this assessment is for your strategic benefit, not external evaluation.
